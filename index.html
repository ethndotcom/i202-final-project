Algorithmic Hiring & the Internship Application Pipeline

A sociotechnical analysis of Applicant Tracking Systems, automated resume screening, and one-way video interviews — written from the inside of fifty rejections and one hire.

By Ethan Pairitz Sophomore, Luddy School of Informatics

This project treats algorithmic hiring not as a purely technical system but as a sociotechnical one — a system shaped by the people who build it, the people who profit from it, and the people it quietly filters out.

Across eight chapters, I work through the I202 toolkit — stakeholders, agency, infrastructure, disciplinary and persuasive architecture, the sociotechnical flower, the black box, classification systems and social analytics — and apply each lens to the pipeline that runs almost every internship application a college student submits today. The personal data is mine. The argument is that the difference between being categorized and being considered is the entire system.
